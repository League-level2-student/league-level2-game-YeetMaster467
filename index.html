Cake Clicker

by: Eli Singer

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

I wanted to make Cake Clicker because I already had an idea for a clicker game in Java. I tried to start Cake Clicker on my own but it was too big of a project, so I decided to make it my Level 2 game. I really enjoy pastries and I don't get my hands on them often.

2. What I Learned

I found out that errors can occur on other people's end even when the file is fine on my end. It was so weird how that happened. I also found out that file size and type errors are not fun.

3. Architecture of My Game

I have many different classes but the main one is CakePanel. It is in charge of displaying all the graphics and a few other things. The second most important is probably Shop. It is in charge of managing all the Buttons, which is another class. I also have the MessageText class, which is in charge of managing that little text bar you see at the bottom.

4. Technical Implementation

My favorite part of the game is the realistic graphics, by that I mean the images of pastries in my game. I really like it for some reason. It takes me a while to find the perfect image and it's even harder what pastry I'm even going to do next.

5. Most Challenging Part

The most challenging part of any project is starting it off. It's never easy to make something from scratch. Making all the image scripts, the buttons, and everything at the beginning is really challenging.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

I don't really know what I could do. Maybe I could turn it into a mobile game and add more ingredients. I could add a whole storyline to it, "The Cake Is A Lie, You Can't Eat It" My most evil idea would be to add micro transactions.
